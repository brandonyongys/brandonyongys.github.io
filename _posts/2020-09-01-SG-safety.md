I moved to Singapore back in 2012 to further my studies without knowing much about it. I have only been to Singapore once prior to my move and that was a 4d3n trip with my friends. I didn't know how expensive the things are, how different Singapore is from Malaysia, and more importantly, how safe it was to the point a lady could be walking late at night alone. It was only after I've moved here and started to learn about all these little things that make Singapore Singapore.

In the 8 years, it has been very safe for me. I could cross the road safely, walk late at night with little worries and leave things unattended for a while without being paranoid that it'll go missing. But my perspective of Singapore safety has changed when my laundry that was hanging out in the lift lobby was stolen earlier this month. Everything including my underwears and my favourite shirts! I have lodged a police report but nothing was achieved. I guess that saying, "Low crime does not mean no crime". Most people are very likely to be taking Singapore's low crime rate for granted.

I'd like to take a deeper look and see how has safety in Singapore changed over the years, especially since I moved. All the data that I am using here are all obtained from [Data.gov.sg][https://data.gov.sg/], published by the Ministry of Home Affairs - Singapore Police Force. The data is accurate as of 15 Aug 2020.

# Crime rate in Singapore
SPF has shared the crime rate dataset in Singapore from 2005 to 2019. In this dataset, SPF has shared the overall reported cases as well as the breakdown of the reported cases. 

![overall reported cases](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brandonyongys/brandonyongys.github.io/master/img/202008-sg-crime/Annual%20overall%20reported%20cases.png) 
The average annual reported cases is about 33k cases. That is the average number. On a tread perspective, you can see that the annual reported cases relatively constant from 2006 to 2010 before it dropped to its lowest in 2013 at just slightly below 30k cases. From then on, the reported cases started to rise again and hover about 33k. However, there is a 6.2% increase in crime rate from 2018 to 2019, which is significant compared to the previous increase from 2017 to 2018 at 1.4%.


![overall crime rate](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brandonyongys/brandonyongys.github.io/master/img/202008-sg-crime/Overall%20crime%20rate%20per%20100k%20population.png) 
It may seem daunting to know that the number of reported cases seem to be on the rise since 2013 and a significant increase from 2018 to 2019. However, this is not taking in account of the growing population. No doubt with an increase in population size, the number of crimes would be expected to increase. When we look at the number of cases per 100k populations, it declined significantly from 2005 to 2013 and been relatively stable ever since. However, it is still worrisome that there is a huge jump in crime rate from 2018 to 2019.


# Crime rate breakdown
The reported cases are broken down in 6 different categories, which are:
1. Crimes Against Persons,
1. Violent / Serious Property Crimes,
1. Housebreaking And Related Crimes,
1. Theft And Related Crimes,
1. Commercial Crimes,
1. Miscellaneous Crimes.

![reported cases by case classification](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brandonyongys/brandonyongys.github.io/master/img/202008-sg-crime/Annual%20no%20of%20reported%20cases%20by%20crime%20classification.png) 
![crime rate by case classification](https://github.com/brandonyongys/brandonyongys.github.io/blob/master/img/202008-sg-crime/Annual%20no%20of%20reported%20cases%20by%20crime%20classification%20per%20100k%20population.png?raw=true) 

A few things can be noted here. The first thing is that it is pretty obvious that a huge chunk of the reported cases are theft and related crimes. This crime category has been declining since 2005. Besides that, cases related to violet/serious property crime and housebreaking and related crimes are declining as well.

However, the remaining three categories are on the rise especially commercial crimes. Commerical crimes have increased exponentially from 2014 onwards. The jump in reported cases from 2018 to 2019 in commercial crimes have far outnumbered the decreased in reported cases related to theft. This would explains the significant increase in the overall crime rate from 2018 to 2019. Considering the exponential growth in commercial crimes, SPF will need to step up on their effort to curb it.

# Major offences
SPF has also kindly shared the dataset indicates the number of cases for the following offences, which are the sub categories of the main categories introduced previously:

1. Murder
1. Serious Hurt
1. Rape
1. Outrage Of Modesty
1. Rioting
1. Robbery
1. Housebreaking
1. Theft Of Motor Vehicle
1. Snatch Theft
1. Cheating Related Offences

It should be noted that the numbers in this dataset do not tally with the numbers in the previous sections as there would be offences in each main category that are considered as minor offences and are not included here. Also, the dataset spans from 2011 to 2018 and no available data for 2019 and years prior to 2011.
